Pittsburgh Black Music Festival
Pittsburgh Black Music Festival
Celebrate the history and heritage of Black Music in America at this free festival
Please Note: This festival has been canceled for 2024.
Music Lovers, get ready for the Pittsburgh Black Music Festival, a free,
family-friendly
celebration of Black Music in
Downtown Pittsburgh.
The City of Pittsburgh is a
world-renowned hub for Jazz music
and the birthplace of many
internationally renowned jazz artists
including George Benson, Art Blakey, Stanley Turrentine, Billy Eckstine, Lena Horne, Earl “Fatha” Hines, Roger Humphries, Joe Negri, Ahmad Jamal and countless more. One of the key events that showcases Pittsburgh's rich jazz heritage and pays tribute to the city's black music legacy is the Pittsburgh Black Music Festival, where the spirit of jazz and its influence can be felt throughout the celebration.
Credit: Stop The Violence Pittsburgh
Event Details
Admission
Entry to the family-friendly Pittsburgh Black Music Festival is FREE.
Dates
Canceled for 2024
Location
The Pittsburgh Black Music Festival is held at
Point State Park
and Market Square in Downtown Pittsburgh.
Experience
Step into the festival and get swept up in the infectious rhythms and soulful melodies of the Pittsburgh Black Music Festival. The three-day lineup features an incredible array of talented artists, representing gospel, jazz, R&B, and soul. Watch electrifying performances, stroll through vendor booths showcasing local artists, enjoy a snack from the food vendors, and connect with fellow music enthusiasts.
